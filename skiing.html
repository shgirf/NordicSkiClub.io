Nordic Skiing at CU

Discover the many ways you can enjoy cross-country skiing with our club

What is Nordic Skiing?

Nordic skiing, also known as cross-country skiing, is a form of skiing where skiers rely on their own locomotion to move across snow-covered terrain, rather than using ski lifts or other forms of assistance. It's an excellent full-body workout that combines cardiovascular exercise with the peaceful enjoyment of winter landscapes.

There are two main techniques in Nordic skiing: classic and skate skiing. Classic technique involves skiing in parallel tracks with a diagonal stride motion, while skate skiing uses a side-to-side skating motion similar to ice skating. Both techniques offer unique challenges and rewards.

Nordic skiing is accessible to people of all fitness levels and can be enjoyed recreationally or competitively. Whether you're looking for a peaceful glide through snowy forests or an intense athletic challenge, Nordic skiing has something to offer.

Skiing Disciplines We Practice

Classic Technique

The traditional form of cross-country skiing. Using a diagonal stride in set tracks, classic skiing is perfect for beginners and offers a rhythmic, meditative experience on the trails.

Skate Skiing

A more dynamic and faster technique that involves pushing off at angles like ice skating. Skate skiing provides an intense workout and is popular in competitive racing.

Backcountry Touring

Venture beyond groomed trails into the backcountry. This discipline combines Nordic skiing with winter camping and exploration for true adventure seekers.

Competitive Racing

Test your skills in organized races. From sprint events to long-distance competitions, racing pushes your limits and provides measurable goals.

Where We Ski

Colorado offers world-class Nordic skiing venues, and we're fortunate to have several excellent locations within easy reach of Boulder. Here are some of our favorite destinations:

Eldora Nordic Center

Distance from Boulder: 21 miles (35 minutes)

Our closest groomed trail system features over 40 kilometers of trails for both classic and skate skiing. With varied terrain and stunning views of the Continental Divide, Eldora is perfect for training sessions and casual outings alike.

Frisco Nordic Center

Distance from Boulder: 67 miles (1 hour 15 minutes)

One of Colorado's premier Nordic centers with over 45 kilometers of meticulously groomed trails. The scenic Peninsula Recreation Area offers trails suitable for all ability levels, making it ideal for club trips.

Snow Mountain Ranch

Distance from Boulder: 72 miles (1 hour 30 minutes)

Located near Winter Park, this extensive trail system offers over 100 kilometers of groomed trails through diverse terrain. The ranch also provides excellent lodging options for overnight trips.

Brainard Lake Recreation Area

Distance from Boulder: 32 miles (1 hour)

For backcountry enthusiasts, Brainard Lake offers ungroomed trails and stunning alpine scenery. This area is perfect for those looking to combine Nordic skiing with wilderness exploration.

Getting Started with Nordic Skiing

Equipment Basics

Don't worry about buying gear right away! Most Nordic centers offer rental equipment, and our club has connections for discounted rentals and demos.

What you'll need:

  • Skis, boots, and poles (specific to classic or skate technique)
  • Layered clothing for temperature regulation
  • Gloves, hat, and sunglasses
  • Water and snacks for longer outings

Learning the Basics

New to Nordic skiing? No problem! We offer beginner clinics at the start of each season where experienced club members teach fundamental techniques. We also organize buddy groups so newcomers can ski with more experienced members.

Fitness Preparation

Nordic skiing is an excellent workout, but you don't need to be in peak condition to start. The sport is adaptable to your fitness level. That said, some pre-season preparation can make your skiing more enjoyable:

  • Cardiovascular exercise (running, cycling, or hiking)
  • Core strengthening exercises
  • Leg strength training (squats, lunges)
  • Balance and flexibility work

Safety Considerations

Safety is paramount in our club activities. We always ski in groups, check weather conditions before heading out, and ensure everyone knows the plan. For backcountry trips, avalanche safety training is required.

Season Information

Training Schedule

When: December through March

Frequency: 2-3 times per week

We organize regular training sessions on weekday evenings and weekend mornings. Sessions are divided by skill level and include both technique work and fitness training.

Weekend Trips

Frequency: 2-3 per month

Duration: Day trips or overnight

Our weekend trips explore different trail systems around Colorado. Transportation is typically arranged through carpools, and we handle group discounts for trail passes.

Race Calendar

Events: 5-8 races per season

Levels: All abilities welcome

From local citizen races to USCSA collegiate competitions, we support members who want to test their skills in a competitive environment.
